About House of Israel

A community rooted in the ancient Hebrew faith

Who We Are

A Community Built on Torah & Truth

House of Israel Assembly is more than a congregation — it is a family of believers who have answered the call to return to the ancient paths of our Hebrew faith. We are situated at Burma Barracks Chapel in Lusaka, Zambia.

We believe that the full counsel of God — from Genesis to Revelation — forms a coherent Hebrew narrative. Our mission is to strip away centuries of religious tradition and return to the pure, unadulterated Word of God as it was given.

Under the leadership of Rabbi Dr. Phillip Banda, we study the Torah portion weekly, observe the Biblical feasts, learn the Hebrew language, and walk in the commandments of YHWH with joy and reverence.

Our Beliefs

What We Believe

The Torah is Eternal

We believe the Torah (the five books of Moses) is the foundation of all Scripture and contains the instructions of YHWH for righteous living, eternally valid for all who trust in the God of Israel.

One God — YHWH

We confess the Shema: YHWH is One. We worship the God of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob as He has revealed Himself through His holy Word, in His fullness and majesty.

Biblical Feasts & Shabbat

We observe the seven feasts of the LORD (Leviticus 23) and keep the seventh-day Shabbat holy, not as law for salvation, but as joyful covenantal signs with our Creator.

The Complete Word of God

We believe in the entire Bible — both the Tanakh (Old Testament) and the Brit Chadashah (New Testament) — as the inspired, infallible Word of YHWH, interpreted through Hebrew eyes.

Community & Fellowship

We believe the Body of Messiah is a community — the household of faith — and that communal worship, shared meals (especially on feasts), and mutual support are central to our calling.

Hebrew Roots of All Nations

We believe the Hebrew faith is not for one ethnic group but for all nations. The God of Israel calls people of every tongue and tribe to be grafted into His covenant people.

Leadership

Meet Our Rabbi

Rabbi Dr. Phillip Banda

Rabbi Dr. Phillip Banda

Host Rabbi · House of Israel Assembly

Rabbi Dr. Phillip Banda is an anointed teacher of the Word who has dedicated his life to the study and dissemination of the Hebrew roots of the Christian faith. With a deep love for the God of Israel and His Torah, he leads the House of Israel Assembly with wisdom, humility, and unwavering commitment to truth.

His teachings are grounded in careful study of the Hebrew Scriptures in their original language and context, bringing fresh insights that challenge and inspire believers to walk in the fullness of their covenant identity.

Rabbi Dr. Banda is known for his ability to make complex Hebrew concepts accessible to all, whether scholars or first-time learners. His heart is to see the entire Body of Messiah return to its Hebrew roots and walk in the light of the Torah.

Doctor of Theology Hebrew Language Scholar Torah Teacher Biblical Feast Expert
Our Journey

Our History

The Beginning

The Vision is Born

Rabbi Dr. Phillip Banda receives a divine mandate to establish a Hebrew community in Lusaka that would teach the Word of God through its original Hebrew context — unfiltered and unadulterated.

Early Days

First Gatherings

The first Shabbat services begin with a small but devoted group of seekers, hungry to understand the Hebrew roots of their faith. The teachings ignite a passion for Torah and the Hebrew language.

Growth

Moving to Burma Barracks Chapel

As the community grows, House of Israel Assembly establishes its home at Burma Barracks Chapel — a central and accessible location that welcomes all who seek the pure Word of God.

Present Day

A Thriving Hebrew Community

Today, House of Israel Assembly is a vibrant, growing community with regular Shabbat services, Torah study classes, youth programs, and annual feast day celebrations that draw people from across Lusaka.

The Future

Expanding the Vision

The vision continues to grow — online sermons, a school of Hebrew, published Torah commentaries, and branches in other cities — all with the goal of returning YHWH's people to the ancient Hebrew path.
